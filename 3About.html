Artist Bio

Charlie King chose to study photography at Sam Houston State University starting in 2019 and is planning to graduate in the spring of 2023 with a BFA in Photography minoring in Graphic Design, Art History, and Studio Art 3D. He will attend University of Houston in the fall of 2023 working on his Masters in Photography. While studying photography in college, Charlie has been drawn toward alternative processes, such as the cyanotype and tintypes. He also enjoys working with mixed media and installation techniques. Artist Statement

My artwork explores ideas of growing up queer in the south. It examines depression, mental illness, vulnerability, and identity and investigates my personal journey through photography, ceramics, and installation. Growing up with depression and a history of self-harm and suicidal tendencies altered the way that I experienced my childhood and adolescence as well as how I view the world. By creating vulnerable work that shares raw emotions, it allows me a place to heal and reflect on events in my life. I share somber, dysphoric, and deeply emotional moments as well as the joyous, euphoric, and happy moments of the trans experience. Trans people are faced with a variety of discrimination both by the government and in daily interactions. My work articulates my lived experiences with mental illness. I create open dialogues about these topics that are often too difficult or shameful to discuss.
